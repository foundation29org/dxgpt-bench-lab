case1:

Patient: Female, 27 years old, resident of Khmelnytskyi region, Ukraine

Complaints:

Episodes of urticaria
Lip swelling
Stomach pain after consuming strawberries, peaches, apples, and kiwi
 

Medical History:

Symptoms began two years ago
No previous history of allergic diseases
Initially examined by an allergist and tested for respiratory allergens – results were negative for specific IgE
 

Course of Illness:

Frequency of episodes: 1–2 times per month
Manifestations: urticaria, lip swelling, abdominal pain
Good response to antihistamines
Referred for extended allergological testing (ALEX test)
 

Test Results:

Positive specific IgE to nsLTP (non-specific lipid transfer proteins) found in peanuts, kiwi, peaches, and apples
 

Interpretation:

nsLTP syndrome is commonly found in the Mediterranean region
The patient has never visited this region
Conclusion: nsLTP syndrome may occur outside the Mediterranean, likely due to sensitization from kiwi consumption
 

Diagnosis:

nsLTP syndrome
Recurrent allergic urticaria and angioedema (Quincke’s edema)
Oral allergy syndrome without pollen allergy
Food allergy
