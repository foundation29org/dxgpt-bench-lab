case 2 :

Patient: Girl, 5 years old

Chief Complaint: Rash and itching on the face, buttocks, and limbs

History:

Symptoms began two weeks ago, 8–10 days after a non-specified viral infection. No previous history of allergic diseases.



Course of Illness:

The rash remained stable for 10 days and did not respond to antihistamines.



Physical Examination:

The rash is mixed in character: flesh-colored papules (2–4 mm) present either on unaffected skin or against an erythematous background. Distribution: face, buttocks, extensor surfaces of both upper and lower limbs. Body temperature is normal. Lymph nodes are not enlarged. General condition is unaffected.



Diagnosis: Gianotti-Crosti syndrome (papular acrodermatitis, viral exanthem)



Follow-up: Parents declined further investigations. Full recovery occurred two weeks after presentation (i.e., four weeks after rash onset).
